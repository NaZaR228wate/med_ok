# Website Redesign Mission

## North Star

Create the most compelling premium Ukrainian honey storefront Medok can credibly own. A visitor should understand within seconds what is sold, why this apiary is trustworthy, what makes each honey different, how much it costs, and how to order.

Success means a distinctive brand experience that increases qualified product exploration and completed orders without sacrificing speed, accessibility, or truth.

## Customer and Conversion Jobs

Serve buyers seeking authentic honey for home, gifting, or seasonal use. Address their core questions in the buying flow: origin, naturalness, taste, crystallization, available volume, price, delivery, payment, and confidence in the seller.

Primary conversion: completed order. Secondary conversions: add to cart, begin checkout, call/Viber contact, product-detail visit, and meaningful FAQ or provenance engagement.

## Product-Selling Priorities

1. Make available honey varieties, volumes, prices, and purchase actions unmistakable.
2. Explain sensory differences so customers can choose without expert knowledge.
3. Establish trust through the real family apiary, transparent process, location, delivery terms, and genuine customer evidence.
4. Reduce cart and checkout friction, especially on mobile and Nova Poshta selection.
5. Use cross-sell or bundles only when they simplify choice and reflect real inventory.

Never hide price, force account creation, manufacture urgency, preselect unwanted items, or make unsupported health claims.

## Experience Architecture

The home page should form a persuasive narrative rather than a template: immediate product promise and CTA; product choice; provenance and people; process or quality proof; delivery reassurance; genuine reviews; concise FAQ; final purchase invitation. The exact structure may change when a stronger concept emerges.

Product pages should answer intent fully and lead naturally to an appropriate size and checkout. About, reviews, FAQ, local landing, cart, order, and thank-you pages must feel like one system, not secondary templates. Preserve high-value URLs or add intentional redirects.

## Delivery Phases

1. Audit content, flows, assets, analytics needs, technical constraints, and baseline screenshots/performance.
2. Establish creative direction, tokens, type, imagery, responsive grid, and key prototypes.
3. Build shared foundations and the home-page narrative.
4. Redesign product, trust, cart, checkout, and supporting routes.
5. Add purposeful motion and interaction polish with reduced-motion parity.
6. Complete SEO/GEO, accessibility, performance, cross-browser, and visual QA.

Keep the site runnable at the end of each phase. Dependencies are acceptable only with a documented quality or maintenance benefit.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The result feels authored for Medok, not generated from a generic theme.
- A new mobile visitor can find a suitable product, understand total cost context, and begin ordering without confusion.
- Product data and business claims remain accurate and centralized.
- Cart, totals, checkout validation, proxy calls, and completion/error paths work.
- All public pages share a coherent responsive system.
- WCAG 2.2 AA, reduced motion, keyboard access, and green Core Web Vitals are explicit release goals.
- SEO/GEO markup is valid, visible-content-backed, and free of fabricated proof.
